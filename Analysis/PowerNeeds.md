# Speaker Strength Analysis

## Overview
Analysis of speaker power requirements and acoustic output capabilities for Chladni pattern generation.

## Key Parameters

### Power Rating
- **Nominal Power**: Rated wattage (e.g., 10W, 50W, 100W)
- **Peak Power**: Maximum power handling capacity
- **RMS Power**: Continuous power output

### Acoustic Output
- **Sound Pressure Level (SPL)**: Measured in dB at reference distance
- **Frequency Response**: Range of frequencies produced effectively
- **Impedance**: Typical 4Ω, 8Ω, or 16Ω

## Considerations for Chladni Patterns
- Higher power enables larger plate vibration amplitudes
- Lower frequency response required for visible vibration modes
- Power efficiency affects heat generation and sustained operation
- Impedance matching ensures maximum power transfer

### Strength

Speaker "strength" is commonly described by the force factor **Bl** (units: N/A).
It tells how much force the voice coil produces per ampere of current.

- **Force factor (strength)**: Bl (N/A)
- **Force from current**: F = Bl * I

Where:
- F = force (N)
- I = coil current (A)
- B = Magnetic field (T)

There is no information about B. 
This means that preliminary calculus will be hard to do. 
There is no indication of the speaker's size in the DIY tutorials.
